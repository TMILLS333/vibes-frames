THE CONCEPT

A virtual gallery where Day of the Dead frames stayed live on the website - building a community ofrenda together.

THE PROMPT

"Generate a Day of the Dead-inspired decorative frame around the provided image. The frame should include sugar skulls, flowers, and use purples, greens, and blues. Ensure the frame is family-friendly. Display the name ${firstName} ${lastName} within the decorative frame around the photo."

INPUT SCHEMA

  • firstName - The first name to display on the frame
  • lastName - The last name to display on the frame
  • imageUri - Base64 data URI

"Generate a Day of the Dead-inspired decorative frame around the provided image. The frame should include sugar skulls, flowers, and use purples, greens, and blues. Ensure the frame is family-friendly. Display the name ${firstName} ${lastName} within the decorative frame around the photo."
  • Basic frame structure appeared consistently
  • Colors were vibrant and eye-catching
  • Cultural elements (skulls, flowers) were present
  • Text was frequently misspelled ("Olie Cater", "sfdsdf")
  • Inconsistent styling across generations
  • Sometimes too generic or Halloween-ish
  • No clear boundaries or composition rules

ORIGINAL VISION

Virtual gallery - a living digital ofrenda where all submissions stayed on the website as a community memorial.

THE REALITY

  • Off-hours project with time constraints
  • Learning Firebase Studio while building
  • Gallery setup wasn't practical within timeline

THE PIVOT

Shifted to keepsake approach - downloadable frames for personal use. More inclusive, simpler to build, faster to ship.

WHAT I LEARNED

Sometimes constraints force better solutions. The keepsake model ended up being more meaningful because people could keep and share their frames however they wanted.
